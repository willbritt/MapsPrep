Maps, Maps, Maps

Mistakes that People Often Make When Creating Visualizations With Maps

  1. Ebbinghaus Illusion
    • Definition: Even if objects on a proportional symbol map are correctly sized, readers won't estimate their relative sizes well.
    • Maps are priceless at offering an overview of your data, but they do not enable very accurate judgements.
    • Ex: If you surround two circles of the same size with larger or smaller circles, your perception of their size will change.
  2. Choosing the Best Projection
    • No map can be conformal and equal-area simultaneously. As a result, designers must find the appropiate balance between the two. Being able to preserve continental shapes and preserve area ratios are essential in creating a visualization with a map. As three of the following elements: shape, area, angles, distance, and direction, will be sacrificed (distorted) whenever you project a globe onto a flat surface.
    • Larger region maps should focus on preserving area ratios more while smaller region maps should focus on preserving continental shapes and angles.
